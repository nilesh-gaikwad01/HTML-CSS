Chandrayan-3

Chandrayan 3 is a third mission in the chandrayan program,a series of lunar exploration mission develped by the Indian Space Research Oraganisation (ISRO) .The mision consist of a vikram lunar lander and a pragyan lunar rover similar to those launched abroad chandrayan-2 in 2019, as well as a propulsion module that carried in the space craft from Earth


Chandrayaan-3 : was launched from Satish Dhawan Space Centre on 14 July 2023. The spacecraft entered lunar orbit on 5 August
and became the 1st lander to touch down near the lunar south pole on 23 August at 18:03 IST (12:33 UTC),
making India the fourth country to successfully land on the Moon, and at 69Â°S, the southernmost lunar landing, until IM-1 landed further southwards in Malapert A crater on 22 February 2024.The lander was not built to withstand the cold temperatures of the lunar night, and sunset over the landing site ended the surface mission twelve days after landing.The propulsion module, still operational, transited back to a high Earth orbit from lunar orbit on 22 November 2023 for continued scientific observations of Earth.

The Chandrayaan-3 mission, announced by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), represents India's continued endeavor in lunar exploration following the Chandrayaan-2 mission.

Name: Chandrayaan 3


Mission Type: Lunar exploration mission


Space Agency: Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)


Objective: To further explore the lunar surface, particularly focusing on the south pole region.


Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle Mark III)

Collaborations: India may seek international collaborations for scientific instruments or other aspects of the mission.
